Stock Tracker

More pictures below

Project Details

Time Frame: 2 months | Outcome: Success

Scope

The inspiration for this project came from a video I saw where someone had a custom circuit board made to mount 5 displays and have them display different things. I decided that I wanted something like that to display stock prices in real time.

Challenges

This is my first project using an ESP32 and an API to grab data from, as such, the greatest challenge in this project was learning how to use both of those things. I started by watching lots of YouTube videos on ESP32's and how to connect them to the internet and an API. Then when I had other problems, I would search forums and google to get it working.

Outcome

The finished product seen above features 5 GC9A01 displays powered by an ESP32 in a 3D printed enclosure designed by me. When the tracker gets plugged in, it automatically connects to the internet and starts displaying current stock data. In the future I plan to add more 'widgets' such as time, and weather.

Custom Desk

Project Details

Time Frame: N/A | Outcome: Not Done

Scope

The inspiration for this project came from yet another video that I saw of a guy who built his own custom desk out of steel square tubing and hardwood he was given. I love a big desk, however, most of the store-bought ones are expensive and lackluster with features. So why not build one myself!?

Challenges

Overall, this design was not hard to make and came with minimal challenges. However, I did design this in Autodesk Fusion rather than SolidWorks. I have been using SolidWorks for the past 4 years during school, but decided to switch it up to experiment with a different CAD program.

Outcome

This project is a work in progress at the time of writing. The desk is fully designed but I need to find a welder that I can use to produce the different welded pieces that are then assembled to complete the desk.
